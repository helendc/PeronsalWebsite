Peer-to-Peer Lending Recommendation

Provided with a dataset of 30 million records from LendingClub.com, my team determined an investment strategy to optimize peer-to-peer lending. We calculated a sharpe ratio to determine the risk/reward value of an investment. Our simulations revealed that choosing 5 loans in each subgrade (125 loans in total) yields better returns than choosing 20 loans in each subgrade. So we suggest investing in a few top loans from each subcategory. In other words, mixing “shaky” high grade loans (e.g. A4s, B3s) with sure bets (e.g. A1s) yields the highest returns.

Local Coffee Shop Data Analysis

Provided with transactional data from a small business owner, my team determined a data-driven business recommendation to help a coffee shop's profitability. Our analysis focused on strategies to retain customers, increase daily transactions, and manage a rather large assortment of products. Determining the cost for each product and thus the revenue each product yields, we were able to make recommendations using regression models. For example, one of our recommendations was that the coffee shop adjust their menu by season to account for changing popularity of particular items. We pinpointed specific items and their respective seasons that would increase profitability.

New Orleans Flood Risk

In COMP 590 Data Science for Earth, my team researched historical flooding in New Orleans with the intention of predicting flood risk over time, using machine learning skills. We collected daily data for precipitation, gage height, tidal levels, sea temperature, hurricane season, wind speeds, and land sinkage in New Orleans from various databases including NOAA and USGS. We plotted this data in Jupyter Notebooks, using tools such as pandas, numpy, xarray, matplotlib, and geopandas. We then compiled all data into one csv file organized by time (a span of 13 years), giving us over 4,300 data points. Reading our csv as a pandas dataframe in Jupyter Notebook, we used scikit-learn's Random Forest Regression and Train Test Split to create our model. We made gage height our dependent variable, as this allowed us to predict flood levels, which define severity of flooding in our project. Our final model had an accuracy of 78%. Our next steps in this project would be to include location variables, such as elevation and distance from levees, to determine relative risk of specified locations.

Sushi Belt Restaurant Game

In COMP401 Foundation of Programming, I created a game resembling a sushi belt restaurant, using the MVC structure and design patterns such as observer/observerable. The final game includes a belt with 20 positions for sushi plates. There are five customers and four competing chefs (the fifth chef is the user, but it is not required that the user play for the program to work). With each rotation of the belt, a chef is allowed to make one plate, subtracting from his budget. If a customer both takes and consumes a chef's plate, he/she is credited the cost of the plate. A plate on the belt will age and be taken off if it spoils based on ingredients in the roll. Each plate has a unique cost based on the ingredients in the roll as well.
